Community guidelines

Keep the support hub safe, useful and focused.

These guidelines explain how members should behave inside official Veyloria community spaces, including the support server, tickets, reports, suggestions, premium discussions and public help areas.

The goal is to protect users, reduce scams, keep support clear and make Veyloria easier to use for serious Discord communities.

Community safety first.

These guidelines apply to the Veyloria website, support server, public support channels, ticket discussions, bug reports, premium discussions and any official Veyloria community spaces. They are written to keep support calm, useful and safe.

Purpose

Why these guidelines exist.

Veyloria is a Discord economy ecosystem with a public website, documentation, support server, command directory, premium information and planned bot features. The community spaces around the project must remain focused on help, setup, feedback, bug reports, economy discussion and safe communication.

The goal of these guidelines is not to make the community feel strict or cold. The goal is to protect members, reduce spam, prevent scams, keep support organized and make sure users can understand Veyloria without being distracted by drama or unsafe behavior.

  • Use the correct place. Setup questions belong in support, bugs belong in report areas, suggestions belong in feedback spaces and casual chat should not interrupt official information channels.
  • Keep conversations useful. Clear questions, complete reports and respectful replies make the support process faster for everyone.
  • Respect official information. Trust the website, official Discord announcements and staff-posted resources over private messages or unofficial links.

Conduct

Respectful communication.

Members are expected to communicate respectfully even when something is not working as expected. Support cases can be frustrating, but insults, harassment, threats, targeted jokes, repeated arguing, hate speech, personal attacks, trolling or intentionally disruptive behavior are not acceptable in Veyloria spaces.

Disagreements should stay calm and focused on the topic. If a staff member asks for a report format, more information or a specific support path, members should follow that direction instead of creating duplicate conversations across multiple channels.

  • No harassment, intimidation, threats, hate speech or targeted abuse.
  • No spam, flooding, repeated pings, duplicate tickets or intentionally confusing messages.
  • No impersonation of Veyloria staff, developers, partners, premium representatives or official systems.
  • No pressure tactics, guilt-tripping, aggressive demands or attempts to force staff into faster answers.

Support behavior

How to ask for help properly.

Support works best when the request is specific. A useful request explains what command or page was used, what result was expected, what actually happened and whether the issue is repeatable. Short messages such as “bot broken” or “it does not work” are usually not enough for staff to investigate.

Before opening a ticket, members should check the docs, command directory, status page and public support information. Tickets should be used for private, technical or server-specific cases, not for every simple question.

Recommended support format: Command or page: What I expected: What actually happened: Channel or server area: Screenshot or error text: Does it happen every time: What I already checked:
  • Do not open multiple tickets for the same issue.
  • Do not ping several staff members for the same case.
  • Do not delete important context after a review has started.
  • Do not move a support case into private messages unless staff clearly directs you to an official path.

Safety

Account and payment safety.

Veyloria staff will never ask for Discord tokens, bot tokens, passwords, recovery codes, payment credentials, private keys, hosting login details or secret environment variables. Normal support does not require private credentials. If someone asks for those details, treat it as unsafe and report it through the official support server.

Premium payments are not active until the official bot release. Anyone claiming to sell early premium access through private messages, unofficial links or personal payment accounts should not be trusted.

  • Do not share tokens, passwords, recovery codes, secret keys or payment credentials.
  • Do not click suspicious links claiming to offer premium, verification, free rewards or account fixes.
  • Do not download unknown files from users claiming to represent Veyloria.
  • Report fake support servers, impersonators, scam links and suspicious premium offers.

Content rules

What is not allowed.

Community spaces should remain safe for users who are trying to get support or learn the product. Content that creates legal, safety, harassment, spam or scam risk may be removed and can lead to moderation action.

Moderation

How enforcement works.

Staff may remove messages, redirect conversations, close duplicate tickets, issue warnings, time out members, remove roles, restrict access, kick users or ban accounts depending on severity. Minor mistakes may receive a reminder, while scams, impersonation, credential requests or harassment can lead to immediate action.

Moderation is not meant to punish normal confusion. It exists to protect the support hub, the community and the project. If you believe an action was incorrect, use the correct support path and explain calmly.

  • Staff may act quickly when behavior creates risk.
  • Duplicate or low-quality tickets may be closed or merged.
  • Moderation decisions can consider repeated behavior, intent and severity.
  • Attempts to evade restrictions may lead to stronger action.

Appeals

How to challenge a decision.

If a user believes moderation action was incorrect, they should contact the official support server or use the appeal path provided by staff. Appeals should be calm, factual and complete. Public arguing, harassment or repeated attempts to pressure staff can make the situation worse.

Appeal format: Discord username and ID: Action being appealed: Date or approximate time: What happened: Why you believe the decision should be reviewed: Any evidence or context:

Appeals are not guaranteed to reverse a decision. Staff may maintain restrictions when safety, community trust or repeated behavior makes it necessary.
